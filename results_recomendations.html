Welcome to your Health Recomendations Hub!

Based on your results from our Health Assesment Quiz you can get recommendations to improve your heatlh whether you are at risk for any Non-communicable Disease that was assesed or just looking for a lifestyle change.

Hypertension

Improve your Diet

It’s a good idea to include more unsaturated fats in your diet, such as those found in olive oil, nuts, and avocados. At the same time, try to limit foods that are high in saturated fats such as fast foods and the like. Additionally, reducing the salt in your diet. It is crucial for lowering blood pressure. Try to limit sodium intake to less than 2,300 mg per day. Also try to drink in moderation. Excessive alcohol intake can raise blood pressure and can lead to other health issues like kidney faliure. Finally, increase your intake of fruits and vegetables. Your body will thank you for it!

Exercise Often

Try to get at least 150 minutes of exercise each week by doing various activities like walking, swimming, or dancing. These exercises can help burn calories and improve your heart health. It's also important to make exercise a regular part of your routine. Consistency is key when trying to prevent or manage hypertension, so set a schedule that works for you and stick to it, even if your workouts start off shorter or less intense. Over time, you can gradually increase the duration and intensity as your fitness improves.
